### 📊 Real Numbers

Over Q1 2026, this operator attributed the following to AI tools (vs. pre-AI 2024 baseline):

- **Time saved**: ~14 hours/week on content + customer support
- **Email revenue lift** (Klaviyo AI): +31% vs. manual flows from 2024
- **Ad creative output**: 3x more variants tested, same ad spend
- **Net ROI on AI spend**: approximately **9x** ($287 in → ~$2,600 marginal profit attributable)

Caveat: attribution is self-reported. But 9x is consistent with what other DTC operators I've spoken to this quarter are seeing when Klaviyo is in the stack.

---

### 🔥 What Failed

Two things didn't work this quarter:

**1. AI-generated product descriptions (pure)**
First 12 SKUs launched with Claude-drafted descriptions, no human edit. CVR was 22% below the 2024 baseline. Rewrote with human voice + Claude for structure only → back to baseline.

**Lesson**: AI for structure and research. Human for voice on conversion-critical copy.

**2. Fully automated customer support (ManyChat + AI responses)**
Tried routing 80% of IG DMs to AI-only responses. Response time improved. But refund requests went up **17%** because the AI handled edge cases badly.

Rolled back to: AI drafts → human approves in 30 seconds → sends.

**Lesson**: AI writes the reply, human clicks send. That one click costs 30 seconds and saves refund costs.

---

### 🧰 The takeaway

The winning stack here isn't the tools. It's **where the human stays in the loop**.

This operator's rule of thumb:
> "AI handles volume. Human handles judgment."

Cheap to copy. Hard to beat.
