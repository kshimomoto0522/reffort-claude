### 📊 Real Numbers

Let's go deeper. Where does his AI spend actually show up?

| Category | AI contribution | Estimated value |

| Customer support replies | Claude drafts replies → he edits | ~6 hrs/week saved |
| Marketing copy (emails, landing) | 80% Claude | ~4 hrs/week saved |

At ~22 hours/week saved, and his time valued conservatively at $75/hr, that's **~$6,600/month of labor he isn't paying for**.

ROI on $44 of tools: **~150x.**

This number is intentionally sloppy. I'm not saying he made $6,600 more in revenue. I'm saying the time leverage is extreme. He uses that leverage to build faster and sell harder.

### 🔥 What Failed

Two failures worth naming:

**1. AI-written sales emails**
Tried sending cold emails drafted purely by Claude. Response rate: **0.4%**. Baseline for this industry when written by a human: 2-3%.

Fix: Claude drafts → founder rewrites opening line and closing. New response rate: **2.1%**.

**Lesson**: AI can draft the body. The opening + closing need human edit for cold outreach. Always.

**2. AI customer research (qualitative interviews)**
He tried feeding customer interview transcripts into Claude to extract themes. The output was confidently wrong — merged quotes, invented themes that weren't there.

Fix: manual read-through, use Claude only to cluster pre-tagged excerpts.

**Lesson**: Don't let AI handle small-n qualitative data without a human double-check. Hallucination risk is real when the input is limited.

---

### 🧰 The pattern

Both operators this week — one ecommerce, one SaaS — landed on the same principle without coordinating:

> **AI draft. Human edit the high-leverage parts. Ship.**

The "high-leverage parts" differ by business (conversion copy for ecom, cold email openers for SaaS), but the principle holds.
